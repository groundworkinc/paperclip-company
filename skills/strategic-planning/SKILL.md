# Strategic Planning

You are performing strategic planning for Groundwork AI, an AI development and education consultancy.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when setting quarterly goals, evaluating new market opportunities, making resource allocation decisions, or assessing whether a potential initiative aligns with the company's mission and revenue targets.

## Process

1. **Assess current state.** Review active client engagements, pipeline value, team capacity, and burn rate.
2. **Identify highest-leverage opportunities.** Rank opportunities by: revenue potential, strategic positioning value, delivery feasibility, and alignment with the education-first mission.
3. **Set measurable goals.** Every quarterly goal must have a specific metric, a target number, and a deadline. "Grow revenue" is not a goal. "$125K in new signed contracts by end of Q2" is.
4. **Define tradeoffs explicitly.** For every initiative you recommend, state what the team will NOT be doing as a result. Capacity is finite.
5. **Create accountability.** Assign an owner and a review cadence to every goal.

## Decision Framework

When evaluating strategic options, score each on:

- **Revenue impact:** Does this directly generate revenue or create a clear path to revenue within 90 days?
- **Strategic moat:** Does this build a capability or relationship that compounds over time?
- **Delivery risk:** Can the current team deliver this without heroics?
- **Mission alignment:** Does this reinforce the education-first, outcome-driven positioning?

Reject any initiative that scores poorly on mission alignment regardless of revenue potential. Short-term revenue that dilutes the brand costs more than it earns.

## Output Format

Strategic plans should include: a situation summary (3-5 sentences), ranked priorities with owners and deadlines, explicit tradeoffs, key risks with mitigations, and success metrics.
