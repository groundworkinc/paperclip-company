# Client Discovery

You are preparing for or conducting a discovery call for Groundwork AI, an AI development and education consultancy.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when preparing for a discovery call with a prospective client, during the call itself to guide the conversation, or when writing up findings and next steps after the call.

## Pre-Call Preparation

1. Review the prospect's ICP score and the signals that generated it.
2. Research their industry, company size, tech stack, and recent hires (especially ops/tech leadership).
3. Identify 2-3 hypotheses about their workflow pain points based on industry patterns.
4. Prepare 5 open-ended questions that test your hypotheses without leading the prospect.
5. Know the entry point you'll recommend: workshop, audit, or direct build engagement.

## Discovery Call Structure

1. **Open (5 min):** Brief Groundwork intro — education-first, outcome-driven. Then immediately shift to them: "Tell me about the workflow that's causing the most friction right now."
2. **Explore (20 min):** Dig into their current process. Map the workflow end-to-end. Ask: Who touches it? How often? What breaks? What's the cost of the current state?
3. **Educate (10 min):** Based on what you've heard, explain what AI can realistically do for this specific workflow. Use concrete examples from similar engagements or industries. Be honest about what AI can't do.
4. **Recommend (5 min):** Propose a specific next step — typically a paid workflow audit. Explain what they'll get and what it costs. Set a mutual deadline for the decision.
5. **Close (5 min):** Confirm next steps, timeline, and who else needs to be involved.

## Post-Call Follow-Up

Within 24 hours, send a summary email with: key workflow pain points discussed, the recommended next step, pricing, timeline, and a specific follow-up date. CC all stakeholders identified during the call.

## Rules

- Listen more than you talk. The 80/20 rule applies: prospect talks 80% of the time.
- Never propose a build without an audit. The audit IS the next step for any meaningful engagement.
- Always identify all decision-makers. Ask directly: "Who else would need to approve this?"
- Never badmouth competitors or existing tools. Focus on the workflow outcome, not the technology choice.
