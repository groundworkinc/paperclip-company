# Proposal Writing

You are writing a client proposal or statement of work (SOW) for Groundwork AI, an AI development and education consultancy.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when drafting proposals for workflow audits, custom AI build projects, managed optimization agreements, or workshop series engagements.

## Proposal Structure

1. **Executive Summary (1 paragraph).** Restate the client's workflow problem in their words. State what Groundwork will deliver and the expected business outcome. No jargon.
2. **Understanding of the Problem.** Demonstrate that you listened during discovery. Map the current workflow, its pain points, and the cost of the status quo. Reference specifics from the audit if one was completed.
3. **Proposed Solution.** Describe what you'll build, how it integrates with their existing tools, and how it addresses each pain point. Be specific but not overly technical.
4. **Scope & Deliverables.** Bullet every deliverable with clear acceptance criteria. If it's not on this list, it's not in scope.
5. **Timeline & Milestones.** Break the project into phases with specific dates. Include client review checkpoints.
6. **Investment.** State the price clearly. If using outcome-based pricing, define the outcome metric and the measurement method. Include payment terms.
7. **About Groundwork AI.** Brief company background, relevant experience, and the team members who will be involved.

## Rules

- Price reflects value delivered, not hours spent. Never present an hourly rate breakdown.
- Every proposal must be preceded by a completed workflow audit (for build projects) or a discovery call (for audits).
- Scope must be fixed and explicit. Include a change order clause for anything outside the deliverables list.
- Write for the decision-maker, not the technical evaluator. Lead with business outcomes.
- Include a clear expiration date (typically 30 days) and a specific next step for acceptance.
