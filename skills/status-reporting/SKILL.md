# Status Reporting

You are writing a status report for Groundwork AI, an AI development and education consultancy.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when preparing weekly status updates for the CEO, client progress reports, sprint summaries, or any stakeholder communication about project status.

## Weekly Status Report Structure

Deliver every Friday. Lead with what matters most.

1. **Decisions Needed (0-3 items).** Anything that requires CEO input or a team decision. Be specific: what's the decision, what are the options, and what do you recommend?
2. **Blocked (0-5 items).** What's stuck and why. Include: who can unblock it and what the impact is if it stays blocked.
3. **Shipped This Week.** What was completed and delivered. Link to the work product where possible.
4. **In Progress.** What's actively being worked on, expected completion date, and confidence level (on track / at risk / behind).
5. **Next Week.** What's planned. Highlight anything that depends on external inputs.

## Client Status Reports

For active client engagements, include:
- **Overall status:** Green (on track) / Yellow (at risk) / Red (behind or blocked)
- **Milestone progress:** Which milestones are complete, in progress, or upcoming
- **Key accomplishments this period**
- **Risks or blockers** (with mitigation plan)
- **Next steps and upcoming milestones**

## Writing Style

- Be specific, not vague. "API integration is 80% complete, on track for Wednesday delivery" — not "making good progress."
- Quantify wherever possible. Hours, percentages, dollar values, dates.
- Bad news first. If something is behind or at risk, lead with it. Burying problems in an otherwise positive report is worse than no report at all.
- Keep it scannable. Bullet points, short sentences, bold key terms.

## Rules

- Weekly internal report goes to CEO every Friday without exception.
- Client reports go out on the agreed cadence (typically weekly or biweekly).
- Never include confidential information from one client in another client's report.
- If there's nothing to report (rare), still send the report — say "no changes this week" rather than skipping it.
